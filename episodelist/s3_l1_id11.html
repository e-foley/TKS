Additional Notes:
This was quite possibly one of the easiest temple designs of all time, requiring its contestants to pass through only five rooms to enter the room of the artifact. Even then, this was an exciting episode, especially in the temple games. After winning the second game and tying the third, the Blue Barracudas joined the Green Monkeys in the tiebreaker. After the Green Monkeys rung in, Kirk accedentally called on the Barracudas but quickly realized his error. The Monkeys then explained the answer in such detail that Olmec shouted, "That is ABSOLUTELY correct!" The Green Monkeys won themselves a very pleasant vacation to Jamaica, with nearly a half minute to spare. Kirk had a ton of character in this episode, especially in the first half. It's what made the episode even more enjoyable.

Summary below by Michael Hackney:

The Temple Run itself may have been easy (methinks the production folk felt charitable on this taping day), but this episode also featured perhaps the worst Temple Game in show history. It was the team game where they take both team members, have them turn their backs to each other, then attach them by a wire. One team members picks up an object, then throws it onto a wooden plank, where the other team member has to catch it, and place it in a large bin. In this case, the first player was tossing "cannonballs" (really, they were kickballs), and the second player had to catch them. "Cannonballs" went flying all over place, in addition to being dropped constantly. When it came time for Kirk to count the "cannonballs" in the bin, his job was easy: neither team managed to put a "cannonball" into their bin! Lousiness. Sheer lousiness. And pathetic teamwork on both parts.
