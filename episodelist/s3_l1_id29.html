Additional Notes:
The frontrunner here was a smart player, and she knew what to do in every room. It's a good thing she knew what she was doing, as she had to pass through seven rooms herself on the way to the artifact. In the King's Storeroom, she found the half pendant, and then signaled it to her partner in case he would need to go in--another smart move. She made it all the way to the Bracelet with forty-four seconds remaining, and finished off the solo victory with six seconds to spare. She and her partner both won a trip to San Diego for their efforts through the show.

I have a ton of extra notes about this episode. First of all, up until the temple run, Olmec was lit with an unusual but interesting light pattern that was different from the other Season 3 episodes. Secondly, Kirk led off one of the temple games by saying, "On your mark, get set, LAUNCH!" The Silver Snake player was still waiting for the "Go" signal after he had said that, so it delayed the team a few seconds while Kirk told him, "That means go, go!" There was also a unique temple footage preceeding the run, that even showed the Bracelet in the Bog during the explanation of that room. There was no audience at all during the taping of this run, or at least, no evidence of an audience. While explaining the temple to the Green Monkeys, Olmec's mouth audibly squeaks. Listen closely for it! Finally, Kirk Fogg does NOT end this episode with his classic "Join us next time for another great Legend of the Hidden Temple!" like he does in ALL others. Note that I no longer claim that this episode was the last taped.
