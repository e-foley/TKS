Additional Notes:
Put simply, this was the most difficult temple design the show ever had, and the players were not fit for such a difficult task. The average temple run requires its players to pass through six, and at the most seven rooms before entering the room of the artifact. This one required eight as a minimum, but the frontrunner added an additional room to that number. From the Crypt, a door opened to the Ledges, which then opened the bottom door to the Pit. From here, the frontrunner was forced to climb to the King's Storeroom and work his way down to the Tomb, where he was taken out. The second player was one of the slowest in the show's history and just made it out of the Pit before her fifty-seven alotted seconds expired, but it didn't matter, as Brett and Lauren had not a prayer in the first place.

Contributed Summaries and Notes:

Der Kommissar: Although it appears that the Green Monkeys didn't make very much progress in this temple run, the temple was set up in a downright brutal design. Even after the frontrunner pulled all three books in the Crypt, the only door that opened was the one to the Ledges - where a Temple Guard was waiting. In the Pit of the Pendulum, the only door that opened was the King's Storeroom. From there on, the only doors that opened took him straight down to the Tomb Of Headless Kings, where he was removed from the temple by a second guard. The second runner wasn't able to make any new progress in the temple, and time expired just as she entered the King's Storeroom. I think it's pretty obvious that the production crew expected a little too much out of the players in this episode.
