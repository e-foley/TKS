Additional Notes:
This temple run is reminiscent of "The Golden Stallion of Ali Baba" (2-1) in that the frontrunner's trek up from the Throne Room spoiled the run. I acknowledge the team for trying to head for rooms leading toward the artifact, but luck was against them; the frontrunner got caught up in a massive dead end which destroyed all chance of a victory. Although she moved at an acceptible speed, it didn't matter--the second player could not even make it through a room of his own. It should be mentioned that this episode also featured the rare slide-out-as-far-as-possible game.

Contributed Summaries and Notes:

Der Kommissar: This episode broke my heart for two reasons. First of all, the British player Lee (from the Green Monkeys) didn't make it to the temple. Second of all, the frontrunner from the Red Jaguars ruined any chance the team may have had of winning the temple. She blazed through the temple, but went up at the Throne Room, and by this move, unintentionally decided the outcome of the run. In the Treasure Room, she lost her pendant to a guard. The Shrine doors didn't open, so she went back to the Heart Room where she retreated to the Observatory - a very stupid move. Eventually she got her act together and retreated to the Throne and entered the Python Holes, but a second Temple Guard ended her run there. Her partner, Damien, raced into the Python Holes, but was unable to make any more progress. Had the frontrunner NOT gone upwards in the Throne Room, chances are she could have set a Legends record - too bad that didn't happen.

Aaron F.: The frontrunner in this episode was fast until she went into the Heart Room where she spent a valuable minute figuring out what to do next. After she finally got it right, she went into the Holes of Python where she was taken out. The second runner didn’t even have time to make it to the Holes. Side notes: This is or is one of the longest moat crossings. This is the only episode where we know there is a British player (Lee, Green Monkeys).
